Understanding the house edge

Every commercial gambling product is priced so that long-run returns favour the house. Short-term wins happen—variance is real—but no strategy removes edge. Budget accordingly.

Bankroll as entertainment money

Only use funds you can lose without affecting rent, debt payments, or savings goals. If losing would hurt, stake size is too high.

Time as a resource

Session length matters as much as stake size. Autoplay and fast markets can compress many decisions into minutes—use timers and breaks.

Chasing losses

Trying to “win back” a loss often deepens harm. Pre-commit to stop-loss rules before you play—and walk away when they trigger.

Alcohol, fatigue & mood

Impaired judgement increases risk. Avoid gambling when exhausted, intoxicated, or emotionally volatile.

Self-exclusion & cooling-off

Use operator tools early. National multi-operator schemes exist in some regions—research what applies to you.

Support organisations

Young people & devices

Myths & reality
